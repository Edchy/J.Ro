# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping audiences, all arriving with low friction and high intent:
- **Wholesale buyers** (café, restaurant, bakery owners) researching a new supplier — they need credentials, contact, and a sense of quality fast.
- **Curious locals** who've seen the roastery or found it on Instagram — they want to feel the place before they reach out.
- **Specialty coffee people** who care about origin, certification, and process — they're evaluating whether this roastery is serious.

Common context: brief, often on mobile, coming from word-of-mouth or social. They're not browsing — they're confirming a hunch.

## Product Purpose

A brochure site that functions as a first impression and a handshake. No e-commerce, no CMS. Success means the visitor understands what Järna Rosteri is, trusts the quality, and knows how to get in touch. The site is the credential.

## Brand Personality

Grounded. Unhurried. Precise.

Not artisan-quirky, not third-wave-loud. The personality of someone who has been doing this a long time and doesn't need to announce it. Biodynamic farming and direct trade are values, not marketing claims — they appear as fact, not badge.

## Anti-references

- Generic specialty-coffee Shopify stores: over-designed product grids, aggressive CTAs, subscription upsells
- Third-wave café aesthetics with heavy brand mascots, illustrated characters, or playful wordmarks
- Minimalism that tips into blankness — too much white, no warmth, no specificity
- "Premium" clichés: dark mode + gold type, hero videos with slow pans, lifestyle photography of people drinking coffee in loft apartments

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the credential.** Every design decision should communicate competence and confidence, not effort. Nothing should look like it's trying.
2. **Specificity over atmosphere.** Real place names, real certifications, real contact details. Details that only a real producer would know signal authenticity more than any visual treatment.
3. **Warmth without decoration.** The palette and materials carry warmth — the layout doesn't need to. Resist the urge to soften with extra flourishes.
4. **Editorial restraint.** Less copy, more presence. Sections breathe. Typography does the heavy lifting. Nothing competes with the images.
5. **Swedish directness.** The tone is plain and confident, not flowery. Swedish copy should read the way Swedes actually speak to each other — not translated marketing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline. No known specific user needs. Ensure adequate contrast on ochre and green backgrounds. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Semantic HTML throughout.
